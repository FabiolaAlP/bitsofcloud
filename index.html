Hosting a static website on Amazon S3

We will be hosting a static website on Amazon S3. The services that we will be using are Route 53 and Amazon S3.
Route 53 is a Domain Name System (DNS) web service that connects user requests to internet applications running on AWS or on-premises.
Amazon S3 is an object storage service that allows you to store and retrieve any amount of data from anywhere.

Steps

  1. Create an AWS account if you don't have one. If you do, log into your account.
    AWS root account

    When you create your first AWS account, it will be the root account, which gives you access to all the resources in the account. The root account shouldn't be used for everyday tasks. Instead, use the root account to create an admin account. This admin account can then be used to create other accounts with different permissions.

  2. Access the AWS Management Console and use the search bar to look for "Route 53".
  3. On the Route 53 Dashboard, look for the option "Domain registration", and click on the button "Register domain".
  4. Search for a domain that you would like to acquire and click on the button "Proceed to checkout".In the next step, you will see the domain pricing options. For this example, I'll purchase the domain for the duration of one year, but you can change this option to get it for a longer time. Also, if you don't want Auto-renew to be turned on, uncheck this option. To continue, click on the "Next" button. You'll have to provide contact information to continue with the domain registration. Once you complete the form, click on "Next". For the last part of this step, you can review your domain registration and click "Submit".
  5. It will take a couple of minutes for your domain to be ready (so don't forget to stretch and grab a snack).Back in the Route 53 Dashboard, you can see the status of your domain request. Be sure to check your email because you need to verify the email you provided during the domain registry. Also, you'll be notified when the email is verified and your domain is registered.
  6. Use the search bar of the AWS Management Console and type "S3"; we'll create our first bucket.
    S3 Bucket

    Think of a bucket as a container for the objects you'll store.

  7. Once you're on Amazon S3, click on "Create bucket".
    When creating our bucket, select a region near you, and under "Bucket name", name it the same as your domain.
    For the section "Block Public Access settings for this bucket", uncheck the option "Block all public access". Scroll all the way down and click "Create bucket". You'll see a notification that your bucket was created successfully.
  8. Now we have to enable static website hosting. For this step, we must select our bucket, and look for the option "Properties". We will scroll all the way down to the section "Static website hosting" and click on "Edit".
    Choose "Enable" for "Static website hosting" and under "Index document" type "index.html". Finally, click on "Save changes".
  9. Back in our bucket, we'll look for the "Permissions" option. Scroll to the section "Bucket policy" and click on "Edit".
    Then, we click on "Add new statement", and under "Choose service" type "S3". Then, we will be able to filter the actions, type "GetObject" and select this option.
    Click "Add" on "Add resource", and we'll add our bucket under "Resource ARN". Then click "Add resource" button.
    Back in our policy on "Principal", replace the curly braces with "*" .
    Finally, click on "Save changes". You'll see a notification that your bucket policy was edited successfully.
    S3 Bucket Policy

    A Bucket Policy allows us to grant access to our S3 Buckets and the objects stored in them.
    Key concepts to remember Principal: Refers to the account, user, or service who can access the actions and resources as specified in the policy. Effect: Determines whether to "Allow" or "Deny" actions when requested by an user. Actions: This refers to operations that can be allowed or denied on a resource. Resource: It can be the S3 Bucket, objects that the policy applies to.

  10. Now, it's time to upload our files to our bucket. Hit "Upload" or drag and drop your files.
  11. We'll go back to Route 53 Dashboard; we need our domain to redirect to our bucket. Select "Hosted zone". Then "Create record".
    Select the option "Switch to wizard", this will guide us in adding our record. Choose "Simple routing" in Routing policy and click "Next".
    Under "Configure records", click "Define simple record". Under "Value/Route traffic to", select "Choose endpoint", look for "Alias to S3 website endpoint". For the region, choose the region in which you created your bucket. Next, for the "S3 endpoint", select the S3 service. Click "Define simple record". Finally, select "Create records".
  12. For our last step, access your domain from another tab in your browser. You should be able to see your static site. Don't forget to clean up, so you don't incur extra charges.
